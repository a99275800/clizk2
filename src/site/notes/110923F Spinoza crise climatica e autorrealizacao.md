## 110923F Spinoza, crise climática e autorrealização

> CRUZ, Helen de. 2023. “How to Face the Climate Crisis with Spinoza and Self-Knowledge | Aeon Essays.” Aeon. Aeon Magazine. August 22, 2023. https://aeon.co/essays/how-to-face-the-climate-crisis-with-spinoza-and-self-knowledge. ‌

> UIUI 110923f #ética #ambiente GLA ok DII no EV no #tescreal; RQRs:  RQR 17R

**RESUMO POR IA**: Este ensaio explora o conceito de autorrealização e sua relevância no enfrentamento da crise climática. Argumenta que um eu plenamente realizado, que inclui a preservação da nossa identidade e interligação com a natureza, é necessário para perseverarmos face aos desafios ambientais. O ensaio baseia-se nas ideias de filósofos como Spinoza e Arne Næss, bem como nas perspectivas da escritora Audre Lorde. Também critica as ações da elite rica que nega a crise climática e sugere que as suas motivações podem ser motivadas por emoções equivocadas. Em última análise, a autorrealização é apresentada como uma visão alegre e afirmativa que pode guiar-nos na abordagem à crise climática.

### Highlights & Notes

Why seek self-realisation?

In the face of climate crisis it might seem myopic but philosophers from Spinoza to Næss argue it is the only way forward

What good are retirement savings if the world is burning?

We need a much richer concept of self – a fully realised self that is worth preserving.

The concept of self-realisation acknowledges our strong drive to preserve ourselves and to persevere in the face of the climate crisis.

Norwegian philosopher Arne Næss (1912-2009)

Næss situates the conatus in a bigger picture of nature, namely, one that helps us to persevere and affirm ourselves as expressions of nature

Spinoza’s God has no overall higher purpose, no grand design. This God is perfectly free and acts in accordance with its own laws, but doesn’t desire anything. Nature simply is, and it is perfect in itself

Spinoza’s metaphysics is fundamentally egalitarian

We are on an ontological par with fish, oceans and beetles

 When our surroundings are hurt, we feel hurt too.

for Lorde there is a difference between safety and survival.

Survival on the other hand, which is closer to self-realisation,

ncludes living out and preserving [Lorde’s] identity across its many aspects: as Black, as a woman, as a lesbian, as a mother.’

Self-realisation implies a unity of acting and knowing: you need to know yourself accurately as part of a vast, interconnected nature, and as more than a narrow ego.

As Bruno Latour writes in Où atterir? (2017), or Down to Earth (2018)

[T]he elites have been so thoroughly convinced that there would be no future life for everyone that they have decided to get rid of all the burdens of solidarity as fast as possible – hence deregulation; they have decided that a sort of gilded fortress would have to be built for those (a small percentage) who would be able to make it through – hence the explosion of inequalities

they retreat into increasingly remote fantasies such as TESCREAL (an ideological bundle of -isms: transhumanism, extropianism, singularitarianism, cosmism, rationalism, effective altruism and longtermism)

The happiness of these future posthumans, most of whom would be digital, justifies neglecting current-day problems.

Why do the wealthiest people seek to actively deny the climate crisis rather than address it?

they are in the grip of bad emotions. Normally, our emotions help us seek out what is good for us and avoid what is bad. We have three basic affects: joy, sadness and desire. Desire is an expression of the conatus: we seek things that bring us joy and avoid things that bring us sadness

because of the complex ways in which our emotions intermingle, it is possible to be mistaken in them and to desire things that really do not help us to realise ourselves. Seeking prestige, fame and wealth seems like it will help us realise ourselves but, actually, we are gripped by them and are in their power

Self-realisation as conceived by Næss, Spinoza and Lorde is at heart a joyful, affirmative vision. It does not start from the premise that life is inherently filled with suffering

final lines of Spinoza’s Ethics: "all things excellent are as difficult as they are rare"
