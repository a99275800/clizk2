## 20190409105134 meritocracia é falsa e prejudica pessoas

> ev 120319; #aeon; fonte: MARK, Clifton. A belief in meritocracy is not only false: it’s bad for you. Disponível em: <https://aeon.co/ideas/a-belief-in-meritocracy-is-not-only-false-its-bad-for-you>. Acesso em: 9 abr. 2019. Versão grifada m kindle.

> suma: meritocracia é falsa porque sucesso é influenciado pela sorte; a mera presença das "qualidades" que compõem o mérito é influenciada pela genética e pela criação[^k093]; paradoxo da meritocracia: em tese ela consagra a imparcialidade, mas na prática testes de campo mostram que a crença nela leva a comportamentos discriminatórios

1. the theme that the rewards of life – money, power, jobs, university admission – should be distributed according to skill and effort

1.  Conceptually and morally, meritocracy is presented as the opposite of systems such as hereditary aristocracy, in which one’s social position is determined by the lottery of birth.

1. Most people don’t just think the world should be run meritocratically, they think it is meritocratic ... In the UK, 84 per cent[^u685] ... 69 per cent of Americans[^s340]  ... /i M popular m td /M[^i841]

1. the belief that merit rather than luck determines success or failure in the world is demonstrably false. ... ‘grit’, depend[^h800]  a great deal on one’s genetic endowments and upbringing.

1. **believing in meritocracy makes people more selfish, less self-critical and even more prone to acting in discriminatory ways**

1. (jogo do ultimato) Just having the idea of skill in mind makes people more tolerant of unequal outcomes. ... remembering the role of luck increases generosity[^d841].

1. companies that explicitly held meritocracy as a core value, managers assigned greater rewards to male employees over female employees with identical performance evaluations[^g124] 

1. This is surprising because impartiality is the core of meritocracy’s moral appeal

1. ‘paradox of meritocracy’ 

1. As with any ideology, part of its draw is that it justifies the status quo, explaining why people belong where they happen to be in the social order. 

1. a well-established psychological principle that people prefer to believe that the world is just

1. in addition to legitimation, meritocracy also offers flattery. 

1. each win can be viewed as a reflection of one’s own virtue and worth. 

1. Its ideological alchemy transmutes property into praise, material inequality into personal superiority. It licenses the rich and powerful to view themselves as productive geniuses.

1. under the assumption of meritocracy, the very notion that personal success is the result of ‘luck’ can be insulting[^s761]
