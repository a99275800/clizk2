## 140621B como pensar claramente (e argumentar filosoficamente)

How to think clearly (JR, DII)

> CHATFIELD, Tom. How to think clearly. Psyche. Disponível em: <https://psyche.co/guides/how-to-think-clearly-to-improve-understanding-and-communication>. Acesso em: 14 Jun. 2021. ‌ IUI 140621B

1. **Expressing your thinking in standard form** means writing out a numbered list of statements followed by a conclusion. If you’ve done it properly, the numbered **statements should present a line of reasoning that justifies your final conclusion** (...)

2. What I find most useful about standard form (...) 
its insistence that I break down my thinking into individual steps, and then ask two questions of each one: (a) **Why should a reasonable person accept this particular claim?** (b) 
**What follows from this claim, once it’s been accepted?**

3. a vital component of clarifying your thinking: **stripping away oversimplifications**, no matter how compelling or appealing, and replacing them with an honest acknowledgment of circumstances

4. **all of them will at some point invoke certain assumptions** that I’m prepared to accept as fundamental. And it’s in unearthing and analysing these assumptions that the most important clarifications await (...) 
Assumptions are those things we take for granted (...) you need to be very clear about **the difference between what follows from your assumptions and the status of those assumptions**

5. While **common understanding and meaningful disagreement** might sound like opposites, they’re actually **two sides of the same coin**

6. This approach is underpinned by what’s known as the **principle of charity **(...) So far as possible, you should **try to extract the maximum truthful and reasonable content from what others say**, especially if they disagree with you (...) 
you should start off by assuming that someone else’s position is reasonable and sincerely held, rather than that they’re malicious, ignorant or mistaken

7. the most important tool in any attempt at clear thinking is the capacity to **test **(and to keep on testing and refining) **your ideas as if they belonged to someone else**
