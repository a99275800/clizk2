## 20190430155223 Lucrécio, cosmologia nasce da ética, não o contrário

> https://aeon.co/essays/lucretius-the-flat-earth-and-the-malaise-of-modern-science

> Ev 290419/a

> suma: [[Lucrécio\|Lucrécio]] afirma que a terra é plana; isso porque, como os demais filósofos gregos, criava a cosmologia depois da ética, e para se harmonizar com ela, e respaldá-la; uma concepção de mundo inteiramente  material servia para sustentar a tese ética de que os homens não devem temer os [[Deuses\|Deuses]]; não havia relativistas morais na [[Grécia\|Grécia]], portanto afirma que tese sobre certo e errado tem de se firmar em algo externo à mente; por isso não se ocupavam de experimentos; afirmam que a virtude está imbricada no próprio tecido da  realidade; e hoje sobram exemplos de pessoas que moldam concepções desvirtuadas de mundo para sustentar suas ideias éticas (ambientalistas e anti-ambientalistas) 

1. Lucretius **wasn’t really trying to reveal the truth about the physical world. His purpose, like that of Epicurus, was ethical**.
2. By explaining that the Universe is wholly material and that the gods are not bothered about human beings, Epicurus tried to banish fear of the supernatural.
3. doesn’t even matter if our beliefs about nature are false. Epicurus is explicit on this point: a completely accurate physical theory is beside the point. **His natural philosophy is intended only to justify his ethical belief that the wise man has nothing to fear**.
4. **Philosophical schools saw their mission as more ambitious than simply being lovers of wisdom** (the literal meaning of philos-sophia). They **tried to promote a way of life that could lead their adherents to happiness**, or at least contentment
5. To do this, they developed holistic systems of thought purporting to account for all aspects of life.
6. **there were few moral relativists in Ancient Greece. [[Epicuro\|Epicuro]], [[Aristoteles\|Aristoteles]] and [[Zenao\|Zenao]], the founder of the Stoics, not to mention [[Platao\|Platao]], all believed that their moral values were objective truths, not simply opinions**
7. **This meant that the concepts of right and wrong needed to be grounded in something external to the human mind**.
8. The **Stoics believed that virtue was written into the very fabric of reality**, waiting to be discovered by the wise sage.
9. Ethical precepts came first, and natural philosophy had to cohere with these moral commitments
10. The primacy of ethics over objective knowledge explains some of the puzzling facts about Greek science. For example, Greek natural philosophers rarely bothered with experiments. They already knew their science was true because it was consistent with their wider philosophy
11. this made progress in science very difficult. If theories about nature depended on theories about morality, and people couldn’t agree about morals, they weren’t able to agree about nature either. 
12. Nonetheless, we are closer to the worldview of the ancient philosophers than we might care to admit. We still find it hard to accept that nature is utterly indifferent to what we think
13. Just like the Greeks, we expect our ethical, religious or political commitments to be reflected in the material Universe. Unlike them, we find science’s autonomy means that it doesn’t always reflect our prejudices
14. On the Left, **gender theorists have refused to countenance evidence of biological differences in the behaviour of men and women**. Meanwhile, **the way that climate change demands government intervention has made it a hot potato on the Right**. **Environmentalists are happy to ignore the record on genetically modified organisms because it offends their notions of natural purity**. It is easy to generate plenty more examples of groups that are selective about the science they support.
