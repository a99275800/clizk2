Loneliness can increase risk of stroke by as much as 56%, study suggests. The Guardian 25 Jun 2024 Andrew Gregory Health editor. Researchers found that adults aged 50 and over who experienced chronic loneliness had a 56% higher risk of stroke than those who consistently reported not being lonely. (...) The study used data from 2006 to 2018 from the University of Michigan's Health and Retirement Study. (...) More than 12,000 people aged 50 and over who had never had a stroke were asked questions about loneliness between 2006 and 2008.
