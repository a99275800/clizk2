## 61W70j custo de uma vida ou custo de uma micromorte preço para evitar uma morte no covid-19 

How do we value a statistical life? ev 160420a. (...) Schelling suggested focusing not on the value of life, but on the value of averting deaths — of reducing risks. A life may be priceless, but our actions tell us that a statistical life is not. The engineer Ronald Howard has proposed a convenient unit, the “micromort” — a one-in-a-million risk of death. (...) the US Environmental Protection Agency values a statistical life at nearly (cifrão)10m in today’s money, or (cifrão)10 per micromort averted. (...) if we presume that 1 per cent of infections are fatal, then it is a 10,000 micromort condition. (...) At the EPA’s (cifrão)10 per micromort, it would be worth spending (cifrão)100,000 to prevent a single infection with Covid-19. (...)  If an economic lockdown in the US saves most of these lives, and costs less than (cifrão)20tn, then it would seem to be value for money. (By way of comparison, each 20 per cent loss of gross domestic product for a quarter represents a cost of about (cifrão)1tn.) [[Tim Harford\|Tim Harford]]
