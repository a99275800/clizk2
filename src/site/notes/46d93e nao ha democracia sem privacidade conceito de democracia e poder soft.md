## 46d93e não há democracia sem privacidade conceito de democracia e poder soft

> VÉLIZ, Carissa. 2022. “Why Democracy Needs Privacy - Boston Review.” Boston Review. November 14, 2022. Disponível em: <https://www.bostonreview.net/articles/why-democracy-needs-privacy/>, acesso em 20 de novembro de 2023. <!--  ev 270421d BTB ok --> 

‌The more someone knows about us, the more they can influence us.

**Privacy matters because the lack of it gives others power over you**.

personal data bestows power on those who collect and analyze

The only thing more valuable than money is power.

**There are two aspects to power. The first is ... “the capacity of A to motivate B to think or do something that B would otherwise not have thought or done.” ... The means can include sorting algorithms, persuasive apps, personalized ads, fake news, fake groups and accounts, and repeating narratives. ... We can call this soft power. The second aspect of power is brute force. ... the ability of people and institutions to “carry out their own will despite resistance.” We can call this hard power**. (RQR 6e6)

protecting our privacy, we prevent others from being empowered with knowledge about us that can be used against our interests.

we should have a say in what others are allowed to perceive or infer about us.

**contributions of Michel Foucault to our understanding of power is the insight that power does not only act upon human beings— it constructs human subjects. (...) generates certain mentalities; it transforms sensitivities; (...) ways of being in the world.**

People’s desires can themselves be a result of power, and the more invisible the means of power, the more powerful they are.

tech uses research about how dopamine works to make you addicted to an app. (...) You usually don’t wake up thinking, “today I want to spend three mindless hours scrolling through Facebook’s infinite newsfeed.” Your desire is produced by the power of tech. In that sense, it is not entirely yours.

If we are going to live in a democracy, the bulk of power needs to be with the people. And whoever has the data has the power. Privacy is important because it gives power to the people.

these days democracy is not at the height of its popularity. Only a third of Americans under thirty-five say that it is vital to live in a democracy. (...) civil liberties and political rights have declined in the past twelve years— in 2017 only thirty-five countries had improved, while seventy-one had lost ground.

“No one pretends that democracy is perfect or all-wise,” Winston Churchill famously said in 1947. “Indeed, it has been said that democracy is the worst form of government— except all those other forms that have been tried from time to time.”

For your rights in particular to be guaranteed, democracy has to be liberal. Otherwise we risk what John Stuart Mill called “the tyranny of the majority.” **A majority can be as oppressive towards a minority as an autocrat**.

**British philosopher Jonathan Wolff has argued that the first step of the fascist undoing of democracy is to prioritize the will of the majority over minority rights. The second step, he argues, is to question the means through which the will of the majority is expressed, thereby undermining voting**. <!-- #tirania #brasis #bolsonaro #novaonda -->
