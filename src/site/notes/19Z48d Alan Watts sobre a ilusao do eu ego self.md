## 19Z48d Alan Watts sobre a ilusão do eu ego self 

> tagpopova tag2020 1n 070520m

> POPOVA, Maria. The Ego and the Universe: Alan Watts on Becoming Who You Really Are. Brain Pickings. Disponível em: <https://www.brainpickings.org/2014/01/27/alan-watts-taboo/>. Acesso em: 9 May 2020.

1. This feeling of being lonely and very temporary visitors in the universe is in flat contradiction to everything known about man (and all other living organisms) in the sciences. We do not “come into” this world; we come out of it, as leaves from a tree. As the ocean “waves,” the universe “peoples.”
2. Religions, Watts points out, work to reinforce rather than liberate us from this sense of separateness, for at their heart lies a basic intolerance for uncertainty
3. Religions are divisive and quarrelsome. They are a form of one-upmanship because they depend upon separating the “saved” from the “damned,” the true believers from the heretics, the in-group from the out-group. . . . All belief is fervent hope, and thus a cover-up for doubt and uncertainty. #fé #religião #fundamentalismo 
4. Irrevocable commitment to any religion is not only intellectual suicide; it is positive unfaith because it closes the mind to any new vision of the world. Faith is, above all, open-ness — an act of trust in the unknown.
5. Just as money is not real, consumable wealth, books are not life. To idolize scriptures is like eating paper currency. #fé #religião 
6. Genuine love comes from knowledge, not from a sense of duty or guilt. #culpa-psicologia #ceticismo 
7. our isolated sensation of self, Watts argues, is our biological wiring to err on always either side of the figure-ground illusion, only ever able to see one half of the whole and remaining blind to the rest.
8. All your five senses are differing forms of one basic sense—something like touch
9. To the central brain the individual neuron signals either yes or no — that’s all
10. these simple elements can be formed into the most complex and marvelous patterns.
11. these simple elements can be formed into the most complex and marvelous patterns
12. the physical world is basically vibration. Whether we think of this vibration in terms of waves or of particles, or perhaps wavicles, we never find the crest of a wave without a trough or a particle without an interval, or space, between itself and others. In other words, there is no such thing as a half wave, or a particle all by itself without any space around it. There is no on without off, no up without down
13. While eyes and ears actually register and respond to both the up-beat and the down-beat of these vibrations, the mind, that is to say our conscious attention, notices only the up-beat. The dark, silent, or “off” interval is ignored. It is almost a general principle that consciousness ignores intervals, and yet cannot notice any pulse of energy without them
14. the general conditioning of consciousness is to ignore intervals.
15. We register the sound but not the silence that surrounds it
16. Space is the relationship between bodies, and without it there can be neither energy nor motion
17. What further fuels this half-sighted reliance on intervals is the way our attention
18. works by dividing the world up into processable parts, then stringing those together into a pixelated collage of separates which we then accept as a realistic representation of the whole
19. Attention is narrowed perception. It is a way of looking at life bit by bit, using memory to string the bits together
20. soon persuades its user that the world is a great collection of bits, and these he calls separate things or events.
21. our so-called love and concern for the individual is simply the other face of our own fear of death or rejection.
22. because it is a hoax from the beginning, the personal ego can make only a phony response to life
23. response is possible
24. the fullest collaboration with the world as a harmonious system of contained conflicts
25. Today, scientists are more and more aware that what things are, and what they are doing, depends on where and when they are doing it. If, then, the definition of a thing or event must include definition of its environment
26. Head, neck, heart, lungs, brain, veins, muscles, and glands are separate names but not separate events, and these events grow into being simultaneously and interdependently
27. he returns to the notion of figure and ground, of a cohesive whole that masquerades as separate parts under the lens of our conditioned eye for separateness
28. A still more cogent example of existence as relationship is the production of a rainbow. For a rainbow appears only when there is a certain triangular relationship between three components: the sun, moisture in the atmosphere, and an observer. If all three are present, and if the angular relationship between them is correct, then, and then only, will there be the phenomenon “rainbow.”
29. Just as science overcame its purely atomistic and mechanical view of the world through more science, the ego-trick must be overcome through intensified self-consciousness
30. there is no way of getting rid of the feeling of separateness by a so-called “act of will,” by trying to forget yourself
31. ego is “exactly what it pretends it isn’t”
32. spiritual exercises
33. when practiced in order to “get” some kind of spiritual illumination or awakening, they strengthen the fallacy that the ego can toss itself away by a tug at its own bootstraps
34. Without others there is no self, and without somewhere else there is no here, so that — in this sense — self is other and here is there
35. Gertrude Stein really meant when she wrote “there is no there there.”
36. Your body is no longer a corpse which the ego has to animate and lug around. There is a feeling of the ground holding you up, and of hills lifting you when you climb them. Air breathes itself in and out of your lungs, and instead of looking and listening, light and sound come to you on their own. Eyes see and ears hear as wind blows and water flows. All space becomes your mind. Time carries you along like a river, but never flows out of the present: the more it goes, the more it stays, and you no longer have to fight or kill it
37. (as citações literais de Watts vêm de: WATTS, Alan. The book : on the taboo against knowing who you are. New York : Pantheon Books, 1966.) (budismo zen autoconhecimento individuação física quântica)
