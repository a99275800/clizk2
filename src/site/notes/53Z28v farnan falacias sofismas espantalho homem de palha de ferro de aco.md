### straw man argumentos 

There are a number of common tactics used to construct straw man arguments. One is per fas et nefas (which means “through right and wrong” in Latin) and involves refuting one of the reasons for an opponent’s argument, then claiming that discredits everything they’ve said. Often, this type of straw man argument will focus on an irrelevant or unimportant detail, selecting the weakest part of the argument.

to take some small part of an arguer’s position and then treat it as if that represented his larger position

It is a form of generalizing from one aspect to a larger,

Oversimplifying an argument makes it easier to attack by removing any important nuance

Another tactic is to over-exaggerate a line of reasoning to the point of absurdity, thus making it easier to refute.

Quoting an argument out of context is another tactic of straw man arguments. “Quote mining
