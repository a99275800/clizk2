## 140621K Chomsky e o consenso fabricado pela mídia

Manufacturing Consent: My favorite Noam Chomsky bit comes from the year 1996. In it, he speaks with a BBC reporter and offers the best summary of the way **the media manufactures consent**. Chomsky says that **people learn to censor themselves in school**. The people **who rise** to the top of institutions **are the ones who speak in accordance with what they're taught to think**. At the end of the clip, the interviewer asks: “How do you know I’m self-censoring?” To which, Chomsky replies: "**I’m sure you believe everything you’re saying. But what I’m saying is that if you believed something different, you wouldn’t be sitting where you’re sitting.**" . (via Perell)
