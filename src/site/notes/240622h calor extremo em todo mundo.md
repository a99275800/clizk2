Well Beyond the U.S., Heat and Climate Extremes Hit Billions. (...)  global dangers of extreme weather as a heat wave bears down on nearly 100 million Americans this week. Dozens of cities in Mexico broke heat records in May and June, killing more than 100 people. (...) Between May 2023 and May 2024, **an estimated 6.3 billion people, or roughly 4 out of 5 people in the world, lived through at least a month of what in their areas were considered abnormally high temperatures**, according to a recent analysis by Climate Central, a scientific nonprofit. (...) Extreme heat killed an estimated 489,000 people annually between 2000 and 2019, according to the World Meteorological Organization, **making heat the deadliest of all extreme weather events**.  (...) Global temperatures in the first five months of the year have been the highest since modern recordkeeping began. That puts 2024 on course to be the hottest year in recorded history, eclipsing last year’s record.
