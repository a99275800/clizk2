Researchers fool professors with AI exam answers. Researchers at the University of Reading fooled their own professors by secretly submitting AI-generated exam answers that went undetected and got better grades than real students. The project submitted unedited answers generated by ChatGPT-4 in take-home online assessments for undergraduate courses. The university’s markers – who were not told about the project – flagged only one of the 33 entries, with the remaining AI answers receiving higher than average grades than students. The study’s authors warned that the results had major implications for how universities assess students. “It is of international importance to understand how AI will affect the integrity of educational assessments,” said Dr Peter Scarfe, one of the authors and an associate professor at Reading’s school of psychology and clinical language sciences.
